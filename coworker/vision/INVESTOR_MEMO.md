# HARD2KILL

### The First Skill-Based Prediction Market.
*Polymarket lets you bet on elections. We let you bet on yourself.*

---

## The Problem

**Prediction markets let people put money on what they believe — but never on what they can do.**

Polymarket did $9B+ in volume in 2024. Kalshi raised at a $2B valuation. The cultural shift is clear: people want to put money where their mouth is. But every existing prediction market is about external events — elections, sports, weather. None of them let you express the most obvious conviction of all: *"I can beat this other person."*

Meanwhile, $121B flows through online gambling every year — and over 80% of gamblers lose money. The problem isn't the desire to wager. It's that casino games are designed for the player to lose. Outcomes are chance, not skill.

And 640 million people play competitive video games — proving their skill daily — but have no way to put real money behind it. The platforms that tried (Skillz, Players' Lounge, Repeat.gg) are broken or wound down: manual score reporting, rampant cheating, asynchronous leaderboards, no real-time competition.

**There is no market where a gamer can express conviction in their own skill, play a real-time match, and get paid instantly when they're right.** We built it.

---

## The Solution

Hard2Kill is a real-time skill-based prediction market. Two players each put money on a single belief: *"I will win this match."* They play. The server determines who was right. Money moves instantly.

This is the literal structure of a prediction market. The only difference from Polymarket is that the event being priced isn't an election — it's **your own skill, against another human, right now.**

Players pick a game they already know — shooters, pool, puzzle battles, fighters — set a wager ($1-$500), get matched, and play a real-time PvP match. The server is the oracle. No disputes, no middlemen, no cheating, no admin reviews. Winner gets paid instantly.

We **take proven game formats and rebuild them from scratch** with server-authoritative multiplayer and integrated wagering. Every game is cheat-proof by design. We own every game on the platform — which means we own the data pipeline, the verification logic, and the user experience end-to-end.

**Live today:** Two games (2D arena shooter, 3D FPS), real-money wagering via Stripe, 5-second matchmaking with bot backfill, free coin play for onboarding. Game library expanding — proven formats, rebuilt for competitive wagering.

## Why We Won't Hit Skillz's Wall

Skillz spent 112% of revenue on paid mobile ads. When Apple's ATT update broke ad targeting in 2021, their user acquisition costs spiked 3-5x and unit economics collapsed overnight. Revenue fell 75%. Stock fell 99.6%.

**We have zero dependency on paid mobile UA.** Our distribution model is structurally different:

1. **Affiliate-based, not ad-based.** Streamers and community leaders earn 20% rev share on deposits from their referrals. We pay only on performance — CAC is bounded by LTV by design.
2. **Content-native.** Every cash match is potential content. "I just turned $5 into $50" clips are inherently viral on TikTok, Kick, and YouTube Shorts. The product creates its own marketing.
3. **Community-seeded.** 1,000+ Discord servers exist where gamers already wager money on 1v1s using manual middlemen. We're not buying cold traffic — we're replacing broken infrastructure for people who already do this.
4. **Viral by design.** Challenge links let every winner invite a friend. Each match is a potential acquisition event, not just a revenue event.
5. **Web-based.** No app store gatekeeping, no ATT restrictions, no Google Play ban. Our distribution is Discord, TikTok, Kick — none controlled by Apple or Google.

This is the Stake.com playbook applied to skill gaming: streamers, affiliates, community, and content — not paid mobile ads. Stake built a $4.7B/year business without spending a dollar on mobile UA.
