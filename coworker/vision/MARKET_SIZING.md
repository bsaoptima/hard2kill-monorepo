## How to Think About Hard2Kill's TAM

Don't say "the online gambling market is $121B so our TAM is $121B." That's meaningless. Here's how to size this honestly.

### Bottom-Up: What Can 1 User Generate?

**Revenue per user benchmarks from comparable platforms:**

| Platform | ARPU/ARPPU | Metric Type | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **Skillz** | $61.70/month per paying user | ARPPU (paying users only) | 146K paying users out of 658K MAU. Only 22% of MAU pay. |
| **Skillz** | $13.20/month per all users | ARPU (all users) | Including free players who never pay. |
| **Online gambling (global avg)** | $440/year ($37/month) | ARPU | Across all gambling types. |
| **Online casino** | $327/year ($27/month) | ARPU | Casino-specific. |
| **Online poker (Italy)** | $881/year ($73/month) | ARPU | Poker runs higher because it's skill + money. |
| **Players' Lounge** | ~$50-100/month estimated | ARPPU (estimate) | Based on $7.8M revenue, ~10K estimated active paying users. |
| **Gaming (virtual goods)** | $1-9/month | ARPU | For comparison: regular games without real money. |

**Key insight:** Real-money gaming ARPU is **8-87x higher** than regular gaming ARPU. A player who wagers money spends dramatically more than one who buys cosmetics. This is why the opportunity is so large.

### Hard2Kill ARPU Model

Assume Hard2Kill takes a **10% rake** on every pot (industry standard):

| Player Type | Avg Wager/Match | Matches/Month | Gross Volume | Rake (10%) |
|------------|----------------|---------------|-------------|------------|
| Casual | $5 | 10 | $50 | $5/month |
| Regular | $5 | 40 | $200 | $20/month |
| Power user | $10 | 80 | $800 | $80/month |
| Whale | $10 | 200 | $2,000 | $200/month |

**Blended estimate:** If your user mix is 50% casual, 30% regular, 15% power, 5% whale:

**Blended ARPU ≈ $20-25/month per paying user** (after rake)

This aligns with Skillz's ARPPU of $61.70 (their rake is higher than 10%, plus they monetize through engagement marketing).

## The Honest Answer: How Much Money Can You Make?

**As a solo founder in year 1:** If you hit 1,000 paying users at $25/month ARPPU, you make **$300K/year.** That's a strong one-person business.

**The ceiling without funding:** Probably $1-3M/year. At that point you'd need a team to build new games, handle support, manage compliance, and scale marketing. A solo founder caps out on execution bandwidth.

**The ceiling with funding/team:** $50-200M/year is the proven range. Skillz did $400M GMV. Players' Lounge did $135M wagered. The demand is real and proven. The question is execution.

**What makes the number go up:**
1. **More games.** Each new game is a new user acquisition channel and retention driver. GladiatorZ and Wasteland are your start, not your ceiling.
2. **Mobile.** 70% of gambling and 56% of esports happens on mobile. You're web-only today. A mobile-optimized experience (not necessarily native app) expands your addressable market massively.
3. **International.** 82% of Skillz users are US-only despite global availability. The skill-gaming market in Asia, Latin America, and Europe is largely untapped by PvP wagering platforms.
4. **Higher stakes.** Your current range is $1-$10. Players' Lounge goes to $500. As trust builds, average wager size increases, and ARPU scales proportionally.
5. **Casual/female audience.** Skillz proved 50% of paying users can be female through casual games (puzzle, card, bingo). Adding casual skill games to Hard2Kill opens a demographic that doubles your TAM.

**What keeps the number down:**
1. **Only 2 custom games.** If players don't love GladiatorZ or Wasteland, there's nothing else to try.
2. **Niche genre.** Arena shooters appeal to a specific demographic. Not as broad as solitaire or bingo.
3. **Regulatory risk.** If states start classifying your games as gambling, you face licensing costs and state-by-state restrictions.
4. **No mobile app.** Missing the 70% of users who gamble on phones.
5. **Solo founder bottleneck.** You can't build games, do marketing, handle support, and manage compliance alone past a certain scale.

## The Comparison That Matters

| | Online Casino | Prediction Market | Hard2Kill |
|--|--------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **What you bet on** | Random outcomes (slots, roulette) | External events (elections, sports) | Yourself (your own gameplay skill) |
| **House edge** | 2-15% (house always wins long-term) | Market-based spreads | Rake on PvP (you don't bet against the house) |
| **ARPU** | $327/year | Unknown (volume-based) | Est. $240-600/year per paying user |
| **Why users come back** | Dopamine, variable rewards, loss chasing | Being right about the world | Ego, competition, "I'm better than you" |
| **Regulatory burden** | Extreme (licenses per jurisdiction) | High (CFTC regulated) | Low (skill-based, mostly unregulated) |
| **Your edge** | They can't offer skill-based PvP | They can't offer gameplay | You combine the money of gambling with the engagement of gaming |

**The positioning insight:** You're not competing with casinos or prediction markets. You're offering something neither of them can: **the thrill of gambling with the agency of skill.** In a casino, you can't control the outcome. In a prediction market, you can analyze but not influence. On Hard2Kill, YOUR skill determines whether you win or lose money. That's a uniquely powerful emotional proposition.
