## The France Situation

### Can You Operate From France?

**Technically yes for pure skill games.** French law (2010 Act) only regulates games of chance — defined as games where "chance is the predominant factor." Pure skill games with no random element are NOT regulated under gambling law and don't require a license.

Your games (GladiatorZ, Wasteland) have:
- No random damage
- No random loot
- No card draws
- No dice rolls
- Outcome determined entirely by player aim, movement, and tactics

**This likely qualifies as a pure skill game under French law.** The same test Skillz uses in the US (skilled player wins 75%+ of the time) would apply.

### But There Are Risks

- French law "does not make a clear distinction between games of skill and games of chance" — both can be scrutinized under gambling law
- No specific legal precedent for PvP skill-based wagering platforms in France
- Penalties for unauthorized gambling: up to 3 years imprisonment and €90,000 fines
- The ANJ (French gambling regulator) is getting more aggressive, not less

### Bottom Line on France

You can likely operate in France as a pure skill game platform without a license. But France is one of the stricter EU markets for online gambling. If you're targeting a global audience, there's no advantage to operating under French gambling regulation — it would only add restrictions.

## Recommended Structure

### Phase 1: Start Lean (Now — $0 regulatory cost)

**Operate as a pure skill game platform. No license.**

- Incorporate a standard French SAS or Estonian e-Residency company (your choice for tax purposes)
- Frame the platform as "skill-based competitive gaming" — not gambling, not betting
- Terms of service clearly state: outcomes determined by player skill, no element of chance
- Block restricted US states (same 14 that Players' Lounge blocks)
- Accept payments via Stripe (they already process gaming payments)
- This is exactly what Skillz and Players' Lounge did in their early days — they operated as skill gaming platforms, not licensed gambling operators

**This works for:** EU, most US states, Canada, Latin America, most of Asia, Australia

**This doesn't work for:** UK (needs UKGC license), France (risky if you're hosting from France), markets that specifically regulate skill gaming

### Phase 2: Curaçao License (When Revenue Justifies It — €40-60K)

**The Stake.com playbook.** When you're generating enough revenue to justify the cost:

- Incorporate **Medium Rare N.V.**-style entity in Curaçao
- Apply for B2C gaming license (~€47K/year + setup costs)
- 2% tax rate on gaming revenue (E-Zone)
- Operate globally from Curaçao entity
- Timeline: 4-5 months from application
- Covers: skill games, PvP wagering, tournaments

**This unlocks:** Operating in 150+ countries with a recognized (if lightweight) license. "Licensed and regulated" badge on the website. Crypto payments. More payment processor options.

### Phase 3: Malta MGA License (When Seeking Serious Investment or EU Expansion)

- Malta Gaming Authority license is the EU gold standard
- 5% effective tax rate
- Access to all EU markets with regulatory credibility
- Required if you want UK, Italy, Spain, or other regulated EU markets
- Cost: €50-100K+ setup, significant ongoing compliance
- Only worth it at $1M+ revenue

## Tax Structure For You Personally

As a French citizen wanting to pay taxes in France:

**Option A: French Company (SAS) + Global Operations**
- Incorporate a French SAS
- You're the director, paid a salary in France, pay French income tax
- The company can operate globally (serve users worldwide)
- French corporate tax: 25% on profits
- For pure skill games, no gambling license needed in France
- Risk: if French regulators reclassify you, you're directly in their jurisdiction

**Option B: Curaçao Entity + French Tax Residency**
- Curaçao company operates the platform (2% gaming tax)
- You remain tax resident in France
- Pay yourself a salary from the Curaçao entity → taxed in France as income
- Dividends from Curaçao → taxed in France at ~30% (flat tax / PFU)
- France taxes worldwide income of residents, so you're covered
- This is the structure many French founders of international companies use

**Option C: Estonian e-Residency + French Tax Residency**
- Estonian OÜ (private limited company) for the platform
- 0% corporate tax on retained earnings (only taxed on distribution)
- Distribute as salary to yourself in France → French income tax
- Simpler than Curaçao, no gaming license, fully digital setup
- Good interim structure before you need a gaming license

**Recommended path:** Start with Option A (French SAS) or Option C (Estonian OÜ) for simplicity. Move to Option B (Curaçao) when revenue justifies the licensing cost and you want global regulatory cover.

## What to Do Right Now

1. **Don't get a license yet.** You have 0 revenue. A Curaçao license costs €40-60K/year. Operate as a pure skill game platform — this is legal in most markets and is how every competitor started.

2. **Add legal disclaimers.** Terms of service should state: "Hard2Kill is a skill-based competition platform. Outcomes are determined by player performance. This is not a gambling service."

3. **Block restricted jurisdictions.** Use IP geolocation to block the 14 US restricted states + any countries where skill gaming is explicitly illegal.

4. **Consult a gaming lawyer.** Before you raise money or scale significantly, get 1-2 hours with a lawyer specializing in skill-based gaming regulation (Walters Law Group in the US, or a French gaming law firm like DS Avocats). Budget: €500-1,000. This validates your approach and gives investors confidence.

5. **When revenue hits €5-10K/month:** Start the Curaçao license process. It takes 4-5 months, so begin before you need it.
