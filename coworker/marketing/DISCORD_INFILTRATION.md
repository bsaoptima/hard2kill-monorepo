# Discord Wager Server Gameplan

## The Psychology You're Dealing With

**The players:**
- Get DM'd by scammers daily. "Free money" triggers instant suspicion.
- Trust their server's middlemen more than any stranger or platform.
- Motivated by two things: money and ego. Nothing else.
- Won't try something new unless someone they trust vouches, OR the money is too good to ignore.
- They don't care about your platform. They care about "can I make money and will I get paid."

**The moderators/admins:**
- Will ban you instantly if you drop a link in chat. No warnings.
- They're usually middlemen who do tedious, unpaid (or tip-based) work.
- They want their server to thrive — more activity = more status.
- They CAN be allies, but only if you approach them as a partner, not a promoter.

**The reality:**
You cannot "build trust over weeks." You need to lead with money. In this world, money talks. Everything else is noise.

---

## The Strategy: Buy the Gatekeepers, Not the Players

Don't DM 50 random players. That's slow, low-conversion, and gets you flagged as spam.

Instead: **pay the people who already have the trust.**

---

## Step 1: Identify 10 Server Admins / Middlemen (Day 1-2)

Join 15-20 servers from the directory sites. In each server, identify:
- The **server owner/admin** (check server settings or #roles)
- The **active middlemen** (look for "Middleman" role, or people tagged in #middleman-available channels)
- The **most active players** posting in #looking-for-match or #wagers

You're looking for 10 people total across all servers — the ones with power and trust.

---

## Step 2: DM the Admins/Middlemen With a Business Proposition (Day 2-3)

Not a pitch about your platform. A business deal.

**Message template for server admins:**

```
Hey — I run a platform called Hard2Kill. It's a 1v1 skill-based 
money match platform with automated escrow (no middleman needed, 
instant payout to the winner).

I want to do a partnership with your server:

- I'll sponsor a $250 cash tournament for your members. 
  Free entry, real cash prizes. You announce it, I fund it.
- I'll give you a personal affiliate code — you earn 20% of 
  every deposit from anyone who signs up through your server.
- You get a $50 balance on the platform right now, no strings.

This isn't a "please promote me" ask. I'm putting real money 
into your community. Interested?
```

**Why this works psychologically:**
- You're not asking them for anything. You're GIVING them money.
- A $250 tournament makes THEIR server more valuable to their members. They look good.
- The affiliate code means they earn passively from every player they send you. Ongoing income.
- $50 free balance lets them try it themselves first. They won't promote something they haven't used.

**Message template for middlemen:**

```
Hey — saw you middleman wagers here. I built a platform that 
automates what you do — holds both players' money, pays the winner 
instantly, no disputes.

I'm not trying to replace you. I want to pay you. I'll set you up 
as an affiliate — you earn 20% of every deposit from anyone who 
signs up with your code. That's passive income on top of what 
you already do.

Here's $50 free on the platform to test it yourself: [link]

Want to try it?
```

**Why this works:**
- Middlemen know their job is tedious. They're not emotionally attached to doing it manually.
- You're offering them money for less work.
- $50 free = they can verify it's real before telling anyone.

## Step 3: Run Sponsored Tournaments in Their Servers (Day 4-10)

For every admin who says yes (expect 3-5 out of 10):

**The deal:**
- You fund a $250 tournament (free entry for their members)
- They announce it in their server with your link
- Format: 8-player single elimination bracket, 1v1 on Hard2Kill
- Prizes: 1st $125, 2nd $60, 3rd $40, 4th $25
- Players must create a Hard2Kill account to enter

**What this does:**
- 8-16 players create accounts per tournament
- They play multiple matches (bracket = 3 matches for the winner)
- They experience the platform with real money on the line
- The admin promotes it, so it comes from a trusted source
- You get content — record/screenshot the tournament for TikTok clips

**Cost per tournament: $250. Run 4-6 of these. Total: $1,000-1,500.**

---

## Step 4: Fund "Bounty" Challenges (Day 5-14)

In servers where admins said no (or didn't respond), do this instead:

**Post in #general or #looking-for-match (NOT a link, NOT a promo):**

```
Anyone want to 1v1 me for $25? I'll play on a platform with 
automated escrow so neither of us needs a middleman. DM me.
```

**Why this doesn't get you banned:**
- You're not promoting. You're looking for a match, which is what the server is for.
- You're offering a real wager. That's literally why everyone is there.
- The "automated escrow" part is a feature description, not a pitch.
- If someone asks "what platform?" — then you share Hard2Kill naturally.

**When people DM you:**
```
I use Hard2Kill — it's basically automated middleman. Both players 
deposit, winner gets paid instantly. Here's the link. I'll put an 
extra $10 in your account as a bonus for trying it.

[challenge link]
```

**Run 5-10 of these "bounty" matches per week. Cost: $25-50 per match (you're wagering real money, but you also might win some back). Budget: ~$500-750 over 2 weeks.**

---

## Step 5: Pay 5 Prop Players to Be Online During Peak Hours (Day 7+)

From the people you've played bounty matches with, recruit the best 5.

**The offer:**
```
Hey — you're solid. I need people playing on Hard2Kill during 
peak hours so new players always find a match. 

$15/hour, 2 hours a night (8-10 PM EST), play real matches. 
You keep all your winnings on top of the hourly pay.

Interested?
```

**Why this works:**
- They already know the platform (played the bounty match)
- $15/hr to play video games is an easy yes for competitive gamers
- They fill your lobbies during the hours that matter most
- They become your most experienced players — they'll naturally talk about the platform in their Discord servers
- $15 × 2hrs × 5 players × 5 nights/week = $750/week

**Run this for 2-3 weeks until organic players start filling peak hours. Budget: $1,500-2,250.**

## The Timeline

**Days 1-3: Setup**
- Join 15-20 servers
- Identify 10 admins/middlemen
- Send the business proposition DMs
- Make sure challenge links + first-deposit bonus are built

**Days 4-7: First Moves**
- Follow up with admins who didn't respond
- Start posting bounty challenges in servers
- Schedule first sponsored tournament with first admin who said yes
- Start recording matches for TikTok content

**Days 8-14: Tournaments + Prop Players**
- Run 2-3 sponsored tournaments
- Recruit and start paying prop players
- Continue bounty matches
- Post 3 TikTok clips/day from tournament footage + bounty matches

**Days 15-21: Scale**
- Run 2 more tournaments in new servers
- Prop players keeping peak hours active
- Players from tournaments are coming back on their own (or not — track this)
- Challenge links are being shared by existing players (or not — track this)

**Days 22-30: Evaluate**
- How many DAU do you have?
- What % came from tournaments vs bounties vs organic?
- Are prop player hours still needed or are organic players filling in?
- Decide: keep spending on prop players, or shift budget to more tournaments?

## Rules of Engagement

**DO:**
- Always lead with money. Give before you ask.
- Approach admins as business partners, not as someone begging for promotion.
- Let players discover the platform through playing, not through pitches.
- Be honest: "I'm the founder, I'm looking for players, here's money to try it."
- Stay active in servers you join. React to messages, chat casually. Be a person.

**DON'T:**
- Never drop a link in a public channel unprompted.
- Never say "check out my platform" or "we just launched."
- Never argue with moderators. If asked to stop, stop. Move to the next server.
- Never mass-DM. Discord rate-limits and flags mass DMs. Max 10-15 DMs per day across all servers.
- Never fake who you are. "I'm the founder" is more trustworthy than pretending to be a random user.
- Don't DM people who aren't active in wager channels — they'll report you as spam.
