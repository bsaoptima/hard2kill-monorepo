# Discord Tournament Outreach Playbook

> Tactical playbook to go from "list of Discord servers" to "tournament booked." Sequential steps, exact scripts, exact deal structure.
>
> Companion doc to `DISCORD_INFILTRATION.md` (strategy) and `WAGER_DISCORD_SERVERS.md` (target list).

---

## PRE-STEP: Have these ready before you DM anyone

You will get one shot per admin. If they click your link and the platform looks dead or sketchy, they're done.

1. **Bonus balance system live.** You CANNOT give free balance until this is built (T-001 exploit). If it's not done, do not send any DM that mentions free balance.
2. **A 30-second screen recording** of you playing a cash match end-to-end — deposit, match, win, withdrawal. Host on Streamable or as a direct MP4 link. Single most important asset.
3. **A landing page that doesn't look dead.** Live match feed (even if bots), payout count ("$X paid out"), real screenshots. If your site looks like a half-finished hobby project, you lose.
4. **Your real name + LinkedIn in your Discord profile.** "Stefan Agrici, Founder, Hard2Kill" with link.
5. **A throwaway test account funded with $50** to demonstrate matches in real-time on voice if needed.

## STEP 1: Triage your 15-20 servers (1 hour)

For each server, record in a spreadsheet:

| Column | What goes in it |
|--------|-----------------|
| Server name | — |
| Member count | — |
| Activity | Messages in #general/last 24h. <50 = dead, skip. |
| Game focus | FIFA, COD, Fortnite, mixed, etc. |
| Has middleman role? | Look at #roles, #staff. Yes/No. |
| Admin handle | The person who pinned messages, runs events |
| Tier | 1 = top 5, 2 = next 10, 3 = skip |

**Tier 1 (5 servers):** 1,000-5,000 members, active middleman system, daily wager activity, admin posts regularly. Get the warm-up treatment.

**Tier 2 (10 servers):** Smaller or less active but legit. Cold DM with personalization.

**Tier 3 (skip):** <500 members, no recent activity, or sketchy (referral spam, scammy admin). Walk away.

---

## STEP 2: Warm up in Tier 1 servers (2-3 days, 30 min/day)

Do NOT DM admins on day one. You'll get banned or ignored. Be a real member first.

- Join all 5 Tier 1 servers
- Read the rules. Follow them precisely.
- Post in #general — react to other people's matches, congratulate winners, ask casual questions ("anyone else having lag on COD tonight?")
- Watch the middleman channel. Understand their flow: how matches get set up, what the wager amounts are, who the active wagerers are.
- React to admin announcements with emojis. Become a recognized name.

After 2-3 days, the admin recognizes your handle. Now you can DM.

For Tier 2 servers, skip the warm-up — go straight to Step 4 with a personalized opener.

---

## STEP 3: Identify the right person (15 min per server)

Not the owner. The owner is usually unreachable and gets 50 spam DMs/day.

You want the **highest-ranked active mod** — usually has a custom role like "Head Middleman", "Tournament Organizer", "Events Manager", or "Senior Mod". They:
- Run events already
- Have authority to say yes to tournaments
- Earn money through middleman fees → care about new revenue streams
- Are reachable

Find them by:
1. Look at pinned messages in #announcements — who posts them?
2. Look at #tournaments or #events — who organizes?
3. Look at the member list — check anyone with a colored role
4. Read messages — who do other members address with deference?

## STEP 4: The first DM

### Tier 1 script (after warming up)

> Hey [Name], I've been hanging out in [Server] for a few days — saw you ran the [recent event/tournament/whatever they did]. Quick question: would your server be interested in a $250 sponsored 1v1 tournament, fully funded by me? No catch — I'm building Hard2Kill (skill-based wagering platform, FPS games, server-authoritative so no cheating possible). Looking to run our first tournaments with established communities. You'd own the event, I cover the prize pool, your members compete for free. Happy to send a 30-sec demo of how the platform works. Worth a chat?

### Tier 2 script (cold)

> Hey [Name], reaching out because [Server] looks like one of the most active wager communities for [game]. I'm Stefan, founder of Hard2Kill — we built a server-authoritative wagering platform (no manual middlemen, no disputes, instant payouts). Looking to sponsor a $250 1v1 tournament for your community. I cover the prize pool 100%, your members compete for free, you get an event for your server. Worth 5 minutes to walk through?

### Critical rules

- Send Monday-Thursday, 6-10 PM their timezone (peak Discord activity)
- ONE DM per person. No follow-up spam.
- Don't include links in the first DM (Discord auto-flags as spam) — wait until they reply
- If you have a mutual server, mention it
- Don't mention rev share / affiliate yet — that comes after they say yes to the tournament

## STEP 5: Handle the responses

**"What's the catch?"**
> No catch on the tournament — I'm covering the prize pool out of my own marketing budget. Goal is to get our platform in front of real wager communities. If members like it and start playing matches, that's the win for me. If not, you and your server still got a free $250 event.

**"How do I know this isn't a scam?"**
> Totally fair question. Here's the platform: [link]. Here's me: [LinkedIn]. Here's a 30-sec recording of me playing a cash match and withdrawing: [link]. Happy to jump on voice to do a live demo with you and a few of your mods — I'll deposit $50 in front of you, play a match, withdraw. You see the whole flow.

**"What do I get out of it?"**
> Three things: (1) Free $250 event for your server. (2) $50 in your account so you can try the platform yourself. (3) Affiliate code — 20% rev share on any deposits from members who sign up via your link. If 10 of your members deposit $50 each, that's $100 in your pocket from one tournament.

**"We've been burned by sketchy platforms before."**
> Heard. That's exactly the problem we're solving — server-authoritative means no disputes, no admin decisions, no "we're investigating your withdrawal" emails. The server determines the winner, money moves instantly. I can show you the withdrawal flow live on voice if you want — takes 90 seconds.

**No reply after 5 days:** move on. Do not follow up.

**Hard no:** thank them, move on, stay in the server as a member.

## STEP 6: Close the deal (when they say yes)

Send this within 1 hour of them saying yes — momentum matters.

> Awesome. Here's how this works:
>
> **Tournament structure:**
> - 8-player single-elimination bracket
> - Buy-in: free for participants
> - Prize pool: $250 ($125 / $60 / $40 / $25 to top 4)
> - Game: [GladiatorZ or Wasteland — let them pick]
> - Date: [propose 3 options, weekend evening preferred]
>
> **What you do:**
> - Announce the tournament in your server (I'll send copy)
> - Manage signups (Google Form or Discord thread)
> - Run the bracket on Challonge (free)
> - Help me coordinate match start times
>
> **What I do:**
> - Cover 100% of prize pool ($250)
> - Send you $50 in platform balance to try the games
> - Generate challenge links for each bracket match
> - Pay winners directly within 24 hours of the tournament ending
> - Set up your affiliate code (20% rev share on member deposits)
>
> Want to lock in [Saturday April 26 at 8 PM EST]?

## STEP 7: Run the tournament

Full flow in `product/TOURNAMENT_DESIGN.md`. Key reminders:

- Be in voice chat the whole time. You're not just sponsoring, you're hosting.
- Stream the matches yourself (OBS → YouTube unlisted link). This is your content factory — you'll edit clips for TikTok/Shorts later.
- Take screenshots of every match result. You'll need these for social proof in future DMs.
- After the tournament: post a thank-you message in the server with the bracket results and a screenshot of the winner getting paid. Huge social proof.

---

## STEP 8: Convert the tournament into the next thing

Within 24 hours after the tournament ends, DM the admin:

> That was awesome — thanks for hosting. The energy in voice was unreal. Couple of next steps:
>
> 1. **Affiliate dashboard:** sending your code now. Track it here: [link]
> 2. **Top performer:** [winner's handle] played really well. I'd like to send him an extra $25 to keep playing this week + try to get him on stream — okay if I DM him?
> 3. **Recurring tournaments:** if this worked for you, I'd love to run another in 2 weeks. Same structure or want to try a different format (double-elimination, team 2v2, longer bracket)?
> 4. **Other servers:** any other admins/communities you'd recommend I reach out to? Happy to give a referral bonus if it leads to another tournament.

The last point is gold. Admin networks are tight — one warm intro from a respected admin opens 3-5 more servers.

## Realistic numbers

Out of 15 DMs:
- 5-8 will reply
- 2-3 will agree to a call/demo
- 1-2 will commit to a tournament

That's $250-$500 spent for 1-2 tournaments and a foothold in 1-2 communities. Expand from there.

**Track in a spreadsheet:** server, contact, DM date, response, status. You will lose track without it.
